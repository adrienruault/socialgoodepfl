Panama papers:
where does the money come from?

Project description

The Panama papers were released in 2015, they constitue the biggest leak of financial information in history before the recent Paradise papers leak. It first came from the Panamanian corporate service company Mossack Fonseca. The leak revealed a substantial amount of illegal activities including fraud and tax evasion. It especially revealed illegal financial activities carried out by worldâ€™s biggest wealth holders, politicians and companies. A leak of that size is a very powerful resource to address the problem of tax evasion.
The project relies entirely on a database provided by the International Consortium of Investigative Journalists (ICIJ). You can find it here.
The main idea of the project is to implement tools to investigate the geographical structure of offshore relationships. It has especially available a feature to query particular items of the database and to display on a map all the financial relationships in which the queried item is involved. Another important feature of the project is the ability to generate density maps reflecting the intensity level of financial activities for countries.
Attempts to create a webapp that would be easy to use for non programmers has been made without success until now. The app works locally but we still fail at serving it on the web.
